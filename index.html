What is a Flood?

Floods are one of the most devastating natural disasters, caused by an overflow of water submerging normally dry land. Floods can be triggered by heavy rainfall, tropical storms, urbanization, and more. These floods can displace communities, destroy infrastructure, and cause lasting economic damage. Understanding their causes and taking precautions are key to minimizing damage.

Causes of Floods

Heavy Rainfall

Heavy and prolonged rainfall can lead to water accumulation, overwhelming rivers, lakes, and drainage systems. This often results in flash floods or river floods, particularly in areas where the soil cannot absorb the excess water.

Learn More

Urbanization

Rapid urban development reduces the earth's natural ability to absorb water, as impermeable surfaces like concrete prevent water from seeping into the ground. As a result, even moderate rainfall can result in significant flooding in cities.

Learn More

Storm Surges and Hurricanes

Strong winds and storm surges during hurricanes and cyclones push large volumes of water inland, leading to coastal flooding. Low-lying coastal areas are especially vulnerable to these types of floods.

Learn More

River Overflow

Rivers and lakes can overflow due to excessive rainfall or snowmelt. These floods can be slow-building but devastating, often affecting vast areas of farmland and residential communities.

Learn More

Effects of Floods

Floods can have devastating impacts on both the environment and human populations, including:

  • Widespread destruction of infrastructure, leading to costly repairs and reconstruction.
  • Significant loss of agricultural productivity, which can affect local and global food supplies.
  • Increased risk of waterborne diseases due to contaminated water sources.
  • Displacement of populations, often resulting in long-term humanitarian crises.
  • Long-lasting environmental impacts, such as loss of biodiversity and habitat destruction.
